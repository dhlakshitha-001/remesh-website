About the Project

REMESH (Resilient Mesh Sensor Hub) is a final-year research project developed at SLIIT, Sri Lanka, motivated by the November 2025 Cyclone Ditwah flood event in the Deduru Oya river basin — which affected over 340 Grama Niladhari Divisions across Puttalam and Kurunegala Districts. The project develops a low-cost, fully offline-capable flood early warning system that operates without internet or cloud infrastructure dependency. All four components — IoT hardware, LoRa mesh, ML prediction, and dashboard — were lab-built and tested at a total hardware cost of Rs. 34,740 (~$115 USD). The system achieves 99.42% ML flood recall with only 3 false negatives on a real 10-year hydrological dataset from the Moragaswewa gauging station.
